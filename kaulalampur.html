Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

An country with vibrant cities, lush rainforests, and diverse wildlife.

Why Visit Interlaken?

Kuala Lumpur for its iconic modern and colonial architecture, such as the Petronas Twin Towers and Sultan Abdul Samad Building. Experience a rich blend of Malay, Chinese, and Indian cultures, evident in the city's cuisine, temples, and diverse neighborhoods like Chinatown. Explore the impressive Batu Caves, the bustling KL Tower, and the green spaces of the Perdana Botanical Gardens.

Top Highlights

KL Tower

The 7th longest freestanding tower in the world needed 3 years to be built, houses several broadcasting companies, and is essentially a broadcasting and telecommunications hub.

Batu Caves

One of the most prominent and unique trademarks of Batu Caves has to be the golden statue of the Hindu God, Lord Murugan, which stands proud and tall at the foot of the stairs.

Perdana Botanical Gardens

Botanical gardens are famous for the comprehensive collection and display of a wide variety of labeled plant species, serving as centers for conservation of rare and endangered plants.
